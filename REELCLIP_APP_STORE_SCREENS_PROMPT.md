## The prompt

```
Create a horizontal App Store screenshot panorama for ReelClip, an iOS video
clipping app for content creators who publish to Reels, TikTok, and Shorts.
A single image 14340 x 1320 pixels showing 5 consecutive iPhone app screens
arranged left to right, no device frames, modern minimalist design with the
following dark mode aesthetic:

- Background: near-black with a very subtle cool tint (#0E0F11)
- Accent color: bright lime green (#C4EF33) — the ReelClip brand color
- Typography: SF Pro Rounded for titles, SF Pro for body, generous tracking
  on the uppercase accent line
- Tone: confident, plain-spoken, no buzzwords, no exclamation points
- Visual style: large rounded-corner cards with 1px hairline borders, soft
  drop shadows, subtle gradient overlays on the accent green

Each section is 2868 x 1320 px.

Slide 1 — BRAND. Centered, generous top padding. The ReelClip wordmark
(green letterform R leading the word "eelclip", same green) at 800px wide.
Below: the slogan in white SF Pro Rounded at 96pt, "Reelclip — make good
clips, Really." Below that in uppercase tracked lime green at 36pt: "MAKE
GOOD CLIPS, REALLY". A single hairline CTA button at the bottom reading
"Join the TestFlight beta" in lime on dark.

Slide 2 — VALUE PROP. Headline in white SF Pro Rounded 220pt: "Cut any
video." Below, a lime accent line, then: "into share-ready clips." Below
that in 56pt secondary text: "Built for creators who publish to Reels,
TikTok, and Shorts. Four cut modes. On-device analysis. Review before
export." Right half of the slide: a stylized phone-screen mock showing a
video timeline with three lime cut markers above a horizontal scrubber.

Slide 3 — FOUR MODES. Headline at top in 96pt: "Four cut modes. Pick one."
Below, a 2x2 grid of rounded cards, each with a 96px SF Symbol on the
left and a 56pt label on the right: "Cut" (scissors icon), "Silence"
(waveform icon), "Splice" (fork.knife icon), "AI" (brain.head.profile icon,
with a small "Apple Intelligence" pill in lime). Each card has a hairline
border on the dark surface.

Slide 4 — PRIVACY. Headline at top: "Your video never leaves your phone."
Three bullet rows in 56pt secondary text, each preceded by a lime checkmark:
"On-device AI. Apple Intelligence. No cloud upload." /
"Photos permissioned. Files picker scoped to what you pick." /
"iCloud downloads are local-only after import." Background has a subtle
green radial glow centered behind the headline.

Slide 5 — CTA. Centered, generous top padding. Headline in 180pt: "Join
the beta." Below in 56pt secondary: "ReelClip is in active TestFlight. Drop
your email and we'll send an invite." A single lime CTA button: "Join
TestFlight". Bottom strip in 32pt uppercase tracked: "iOS 17+ · on-device
by default · made for creators who cut for Reels, TikTok, Shorts."
```

---

## Why these five slides

- **Slide 1 (Brand)** — the App Store first impression. Wordmark + slogan does the discovery work in the 1.5s a scroller spends on a screenshot.
- **Slide 2 (Value prop)** — the H1 of the website. "Cut any video into share-ready clips" is the single most tested claim; the slide is the conversion moment for the scroll.
- **Slide 3 (Four modes)** — the product surface. Showing the mode picker proves this isn't a single-feature app.
- **Slide 4 (Privacy)** — the differentiator vs Descript / Opus Clip. Cloud-AI clipping tools can't make this claim.
- **Slide 5 (CTA)** — the bottom-of-page close. "Join the beta" is the same primary action as the website hero.

---

## Variants to A/B if the generator supports it

- **Slide 1 alt:** Replace the CTA button with a single product screenshot (home screen showing the Projects list) — proves the app is real.
- **Slide 3 alt:** Drop the 2x2 grid, instead show a single Cut-mode timeline with the result clips stacked below — more "show, don't tell".
- **Slide 4 alt:** Lead with the iconography ("a phone, a shield, a checkmark in green") and put the bullets below — visual-first, text-second.
